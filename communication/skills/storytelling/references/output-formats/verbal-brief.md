# Output Format: Verbal Brief

## What it is

A speaking script for 5–10 minute verbal presentations. Not meant to be read aloud — meant to be practiced. It structures what to say, in what order, with what transitions, and how to open space for reaction.

## When to use

- Executive meetings where you have 10 minutes on the calendar
- Presentation without slides (or slides as minimal support)
- 1:1 with leadership where you need to recommend something
- Quick all-hands or session opening
- Any situation where improvising is risky

## Template

```markdown
# VERBAL BRIEF: [topic in 1 sentence]

**Audience:** [who]
**Time available:** [X minutes]
**Objective:** [approval / alignment / information]
**What I need from them:** [decision, feedback, ok to proceed]

---

## OPENING (30–60 seconds)

[Hook — question, data point, or short story]

Script:
"[Exact opening text. Concrete, specific, no preamble.]"

Tip: do not start with "So, what I want to present today is..."
Start with the problem or the story.

---

## CONTEXT (30–60 seconds)

Script:
"[What the audience needs to know — max 2 sentences]"

Transition:
"And the problem that creates is..."

---

## PROBLEM (60 seconds)

Script:
"[The specific problem, with data if available]"

Transition:
"That's why I recommend..." or "What I'm proposing is..."

---

## RECOMMENDATION (60–90 seconds)

Script:
"[The recommendation in 1 sentence, then the 2–3 main arguments]"

If using supporting slides: "[Here you can see that...]"

Transition:
"To get to this recommendation, we considered [X alternatives]..."

---

## RISKS (30 seconds)

Script:
"The main risk is [X]. The mitigation is [Y]."

---

## NEXT STEPS + CALL TO ACTION (30–60 seconds)

Script:
"If approved today, the next step is [concrete action + owner + deadline]."

Opening for reaction:
"I'd like to hear from you — what's your main question or concern?"

---

## EXPECTED OBJECTIONS (prepare, do not say — for use in Q&A)

| Objection | Prepared response |
|---|---|
| "[Objection 1]" | "[Response]" |
| "[Objection 2]" | "[Response]" |
| "[Objection 3]" | "I don't have an answer right now — I can confirm by [date]" |
```

## Delivery rules (not about text — about presence)

- **Pause after the opening** — let the hook breathe
- **Pace** — slower than it seems necessary
- **"I don't know"** — said with confidence is worth more than an insecure improvisation
- **Clock awareness** — if time runs out: "I have two more points — should I continue or would you prefer I send it in writing?"

## Integration with other outputs

- Have an exec-memo? The verbal brief is the oral summary of the memo — it doesn't repeat everything, it references it
- Have a deck? The verbal brief is the speaking script slide by slide — each section of the brief = one slide
- Verbal only? The brief is the complete document — it needs to cover Context + Problem + Recommendation + Risks + CTA
