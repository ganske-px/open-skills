# JTBD Interview Guide

## Before the Interview

**Required input:**
- Which job/situation do you want to explore? (e.g., "evaluating a new vendor", "onboarding a service provider")
- What is the interviewee profile? (e.g., operations manager, procurement lead, end user)
- How many interviews are planned?

**Goal of the JTBD interview:**
Not to validate a solution. The goal is to understand the job the customer is trying to accomplish, the moments that trigger that job, and the forces that influence their decision to adopt or reject a solution.

---

## 4-Phase Script

### Phase 1 — Situation Context (10 min)

Goal: understand WHEN the job happens, not who the person is.

Questions:
- "Tell me about the last time you had to [relevant situation]. What happened?"
- "What was going on in your day when that started?"
- "What made you realize you needed to deal with it at that moment?"
- "What did the process look like before you had a solution for this?"

**Signal it's working:** the interviewee describes a specific past situation, not a generic hypothetical.

---

### Phase 2 — Motivation and Progress (15 min)

Goal: understand what the customer wants to ACCOMPLISH, separate from the solution they use today.

Questions:
- "What were you trying to achieve at that moment?"
- "What was the ideal outcome for you?"
- "If everything had gone perfectly, what would that look like?"
- "What would be resolved if you could do this well?"
- "What happens if you DON'T manage to do this?"

**Signal it's working:** the interviewee describes an outcome, not a feature.

---

### Phase 3 — Forces of Progress (15 min)

Goal: understand what drives change and what holds it back.

**Push (frustration with the current state):**
- "What bothered you about the way you did this before?"
- "What made you think there had to be a better way?"

**Pull (attraction to the new solution):**
- "What caught your attention when you first came across [solution]?"
- "What made you think it was worth trying?"

**Anxiety (fear of changing):**
- "What were you worried about when you considered switching?"
- "What could go wrong?"
- "What did you need to confirm before committing?"

**Habit (comfort with current behavior):**
- "What did you like about the old way?"
- "What did you give up when you switched?"
- "Is there anything you still do the old way even with the new solution?"

---

### Phase 4 — Hiring / Firing (10 min)

Goal: understand what was "hired" and "fired" — and why.

**Big Hire (adoption decision):**
- "When you decided to use [solution] for the first time, what weighed most in your decision?"
- "What would have stopped you from adopting it?"

**Little Hire (continued use):**
- "When you open [solution], what typically made you open it?"
- "Are there moments when you prefer not to use it and do it another way?"

**Firing:**
- "Has there ever been a moment when you considered stopping? What happened?"
- "If you were going to replace [solution] tomorrow, what would you need to see to make the switch?"
